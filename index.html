Welcome to Lake Tapps!

This large, manmade reservoir lies just 10 miles east of Tacoma, WA. The first thing that many people notice when looking at this lake on a map is its strange shape. In fact, despite having an area of only 4.5 square miles, Lake Tapps has around 45 miles of shoreline. To put this number into perspective, Lake Washington in Seattle has an area of around 33.8 square miles and only 50 miles of shoreline.

This complex shape has proven ideal for the construction of waterfront homes, and today, nearly all of the lake’s 45 miles of shoreline is private residential land. That is, with the notable exception of the lake’s two public parks, Allan Yorke and North Lake Tapps Park. However, this wasn’t always the case, and despite being enveloped in monotonous suburban sprawl, Lake Tapps has a deep and surprisingly interesting history. To properly tell it, we will have to travel back to the turn of the century.
